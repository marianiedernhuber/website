Biography

I am a postdoctoral researcher in cognitive neuroscience in the Consciousness and Cognition Lab, University of Cambridge (with Tristan Bekinschtein) and at the Institute of Cognitive Neuroscience: Body, Self, Plasticity Lab (with Bigna Lenggenhager). My goal is to apply insights from cognitive neuroscience and technology to assist patient rehabilitation. My current work is focused on the development of data-driven tools which uses insights from cognitive science, neurobiology, medicine and patient experience to improve quality of life for chronic pain patients. Beyond that, I have a special interest in diversity and the empowerment of minorities.

Featured Publications

Maria Niedernhuber, Damiano G. Barone, Bigna Lenggenhager
September 2019 Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews, 92
Prostheses as extensions of the body - Progress and challenges

Prosthesis incorporation faces challenges in basic neuroscience and clinical reality. Here we describe cognitive and sensory procedures with potential for prosthetics incorporation. We review their implications for the development of incorporated neurotechnologies. Little evidence suggests that current methods improve prosthetics incorporation. We present several milestones to be reached towards incorporating prostheses.
